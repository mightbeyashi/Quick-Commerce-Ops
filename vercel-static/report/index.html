Business Problem

Quick-commerce customers expect products to be available immediately. Stockouts can reduce conversion, hurt trust, and push customers to substitute or leave. The goal of this project is to help an operations or category team identify which SKUs and categories need attention first.

  1. Find categories with weak availability.
  2. Rank out-of-stock and low-stock SKUs by operational priority.
  3. Highlight high-discount products where discount exposure is concentrated.
  4. Prepare clean tables for Excel and Power BI.

How To Explain This In Interview

  • Problem: quick-commerce availability and replenishment prioritization.
  • Data: 3.7K+ SKU inventory records with category, price, discount, quantity, weight, and stock flag.
  • SQL: clean inventory view, category health view, discount exposure view, and replenishment watchlist.
  • Python: encoding fix, paise-to-rupee conversion, EDA, scoring, charts, and exports.
  • Excel: CSV outputs for pivot tables, lookups, conditional formatting, and what-if analysis.
  • Decision: prioritize unavailable or low-stock high-value SKUs in categories with weak availability.

Limitations

This is an inventory snapshot, not a full company warehouse. It lacks demand, orders, delivery time, gross margin, dark-store ID, lead time, and expiry data. With those fields, the watchlist could be upgraded into a true lost-sales and replenishment model.
