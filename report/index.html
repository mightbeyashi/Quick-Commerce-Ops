Business Problem

Quick-commerce customers expect products to be available immediately. Stockouts can reduce conversion, hurt trust, and push customers to substitute or leave. The goal of this project is to help an operations or category team identify which SKUs and categories need attention first.

  1. Find categories with weak availability.
  2. Rank out-of-stock and low-stock SKUs by operational priority.
  3. Highlight high-discount products where discount exposure is concentrated.
  4. Estimate what a Product Analyst would measure if order data were available.
  5. Prepare clean tables for Excel, SQL practice, and Power BI dashboards.

How To Explain This In Interview

  • Problem: quick-commerce availability and replenishment prioritization.
  • Data: 3.7K+ observed SKU inventory rows with category, price, discount, quantity, weight, and stock flag.
  • Data honesty: the dataset has no true orders, so Product Analyst metrics are scenario estimates, not actual company performance.
  • SQL: clean inventory view, category health view, discount exposure view, and replenishment watchlist.
  • Python: encoding fix, paise-to-rupee conversion, EDA, scoring, scenario simulation, charts, and exports.
  • Excel/Power BI: CSV outputs for dashboards, pivot tables, conditional formatting, and what-if analysis.
  • Product thinking: define fill rate, unfulfilled value, substitution acceptance, experiment metrics, and guardrails.

Limitations

This is an inventory snapshot, not a full company warehouse. It lacks actual demand, historical orders, gross margin, replenishment lead time, expiry, and real dark-store operations. Because of that, the report separates observed inventory facts from scenario-estimated product metrics.
